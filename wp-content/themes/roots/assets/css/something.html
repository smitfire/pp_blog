Our Managing Trust Account Risk session in Cape Town this morning was a huge success – and judging from the responses after the event, also extremely insightful! It's fantastic that even seasoned estate agents can still learn a thing or two about mitigating risk in this context. One of the highlights of the morning was the presentation by Bryan Chaplog, acting CEO of the EAAB, who set out the regulatory and compliance landscape of this area of an estate agent's businesses. His talk was followed by Jaco Odendaal, director at the Exceed Group, who discussed audit requirements in a trust account setting and the pitfalls of dealing with public funds. Andre Holtshausen, CEO of PayProp Group Services, then explained how the PayProp trust environment conforms to audit and regulatory requirements. As we build up to the Johannesburg and Durban sessions, we strongly recommend attendees bring their auditor along for an exceedingly useful and reassuring briefing! In addition to these indispensable talks, Marlon Shevelew from Marlon Shevelew and Associates delivered a brilliant and typically infectious overview of the legal obligations of estate agents who manage trust accounts – an important aspect that is often skimmed over. And to end matters off with a bang, Faffa Knoetze, CEO of PayProp Capital, introduced a brand-new way of reducing risk in your damage deposit account. We could tell you, but we’d rather do it in person in more conducive circumstances. Join us for either session and be sure to grab a full presentation pack for all the details!
